UNDERSTANDING THE TWO USER TYPES
================================================================================

Your system has TWO types of users:

1. STAFF/ADMIN USERS (profiles table)
   - Field Officers
   - Branch Managers
   - Area Managers
   - Admins
   - These are EMPLOYEES who work for Alwan

2. BORROWERS/CLIENTS (members table)
   - Regular people who apply for loans
   - They sign up through the website
   - They go through KYC process
   - These are CUSTOMERS of Alwan


PROFILES TABLE - For Staff/Employees
================================================================================

Purpose: Stores information about Alwan STAFF members (not borrowers)

Fields:
- id: Links to auth.users (staff login account)
- email: Staff email
- full_name: Staff name
- role: field_officer, branch_manager, area_manager, admin
- area_id: Which area they manage
- branch_id: Which branch they work at
- phone: Staff contact number

Who uses this:
- Field officers who collect payments
- Branch managers who oversee branches
- Area managers who oversee multiple branches
- Admins who approve loans and manage system


MEMBERS TABLE - For Borrowers/Clients
================================================================================

Purpose: Stores information about BORROWERS (people who get loans)

Fields:
- id: Unique member ID
- center_id: Which center they belong to
- first_name, last_name, middle_name: Member's name
- date_of_birth, gender: Personal info
- phone, address: Contact info
- business_name, business_type: Their business
- cbu_balance: Capital Build-Up savings
- kyc_status: KYC verification status

Who uses this:
- Regular people who sign up on the website
- They apply for loans
- They make weekly payments
- They are the CUSTOMERS


DUPLICATED FIELDS - Why They Exist
================================================================================

You noticed some fields appear in both members and member_profiles:

MEMBERS table has:
- kyc_status
- kyc_level  
- email
- civil_status
- nationality

MEMBER_PROFILES table has:
- civil_status
- nationality
- email

WHY THE DUPLICATION?

The members table was created FIRST with basic fields.
Later, member_profiles was added for EXTENDED information.

This is actually REDUNDANT and should be cleaned up!


RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE
================================================================================

OPTION 1: Keep it simple (Recommended)
---------------------------------------
Remove member_profiles table entirely and keep everything in members table.

Pros:
- Simpler queries
- No joins needed
- Easier to understand
- Less confusion

Cons:
- Members table gets bigger
- But this is fine for your use case


OPTION 2: Separate concerns (More complex)
-------------------------------------------
Keep member_profiles but remove duplicated fields from members table.

Members table: Basic info only
- id, center_id, first_name, last_name
- phone, address, business info
- cbu_balance, is_active

Member_profiles table: Extended KYC info
- civil_status, nationality, email
- mothers_maiden_name, dependents
- tin_number, place_of_birth


CURRENT ISSUES IN YOUR SCHEMA
================================================================================

1. DUPLICATED FIELDS
   - email exists in both members and member_profiles
   - civil_status exists in both
   - nationality exists in both
   
   FIX: Remove from one table (recommend keeping in members)

2. MISSING UNIQUE CONSTRAINT
   - members.phone should be UNIQUE
   - members.email should be UNIQUE (if used)
   
   FIX: Add unique constraints

3. INCONSISTENT NAMING
   - Some tables use snake_case (member_profiles)
   - Some use camelCase in code
   
   FIX: Stick to snake_case in database

FLOW EXPLANATION
================================================================================

1. STAFF SIGNUP (Manual by Admin)
   auth.users created → profiles created → assigned to branch/area

2. BORROWER SIGNUP (Website)
   auth.users created → kyc_applications created → 
   admin approves → members created → all member_* tables populated

3. LOAN APPLICATION
   member applies → loans created → 
   staff approves → repayment_schedules created

4. WEEKLY PAYMENTS
   field officer collects → transactions created → 
   loan balance updated → cbu_balance updated
